Datasets in upcoming year

The city tech plan and this report identified the expansion of new datasets as the primary goal. The Open Data Coordinators worked with their respective departments to identify new and pertinant data for the portal. An exciting list of datasets have been identified as targets for the upcoming year, including the release of 911 calls, 311 calls, fire inspection reports, automobile accidents, and data on the city-owned fleet, buildings, and leases.

911 Calls

The list of crimes in Chicago from 2001 to present is one of the most powerful and most-viewed datasets on the portal. It provides valuable information on public safety while providing resource for researchers throughout the country. The city will continue to provide valuable public safety data by releasing deanonymized 911 calls logs on the portal. The logs will include calls for paramedics, fire and police departments.

311 Calls

Chicago has released select 311 data. Twelve service request types, including calls to fix potholes and to cleaning graffiti. However, there are hundreds of calls for service around Chicago. During the upcoming year, Chicago will release all public 311 calls on the data portal. These calls will include rodent sightings, illegal business complaints, aircraft noise complaints, and complaints on improper heating or cooling in apartment buildings.
